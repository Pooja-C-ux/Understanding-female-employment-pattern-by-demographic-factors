# Understanding female employment patterns in India: A cross sectional study
#

Presenting a cross-sectional study on the female employment patterns in India using PLFS January-December 2024 dataset. 



### Data and Methodology 

-Pulled PLFS dataset from MOSPI website. Cleaned and harmonized data columns. 

-Recoded categorical variables and created different demographical categories like age category, educational category and type of employment. 

-Estimated labour market indicators -namely labour force participation rate, unemployment rate and worker population rate.

-Produced summary tables by different demographic factors.

-Used data visualisation tools to perform graphical and tabular analysis to draw insights. 



### Key Insights

-Female employment is higher in rural areas than in urban areas, but they are largely employed as unpaid family worker or as casual labour in rural areas.

-In contrast overall female employment is lower 

-Higher unemployment rate among young female workers with higher education (between the range 31 to 39 percent).

-U shaped relationship between female labour force participation rate and educational level

-Huge gender gap in employment rate between male and female with same level of education

-Large proportion of female population attend to only domestic duties (42. 8 percent).



Policy Insight

-Improve quality of employment, creating employment opportunities matching skill levels and provide important facilities like childcare facility and safe and secure workplace.
